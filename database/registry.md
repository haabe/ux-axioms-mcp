* **Anchoring Bias:** The tendency to rely too heavily on the first piece of information offered (the "anchor") when making decisions.
* **The Pratfall Effect:** People's perceived attractiveness increases when they make a mistakeâ€”provided they are perceived as competent.
* **Social Proof:** People look to others' behaviors to guide their own actions, especially in uncertain situations.
* **Scarcity Principle:** The perception that products or services are more attractive when their availability is limited.
* **Loss Aversion:** The pain of losing is psychologically twice as powerful as the pleasure of gaining.
* **Decoy Effect:** Consumers tend to have a specific change in preference between two options when presented with a third, asymmetrical option.

* **Recency Bias:** More weight is given to recent experiences.
* **Social Desirability Bias:** People provide socially acceptable answers.
* **False-Consensus Bias:** We overestimate how much others agree with us.
* **The Curse of Knowledge:** Experts assume others have the same knowledge.
* **Clustering Illusion:** Seeing patterns in random data.
* **Sunk Cost Fallacy:** Continuing an investment because of past costs.
